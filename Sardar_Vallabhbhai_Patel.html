Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

            This man is a great leader who united our country

            

            Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement and played a vital role in the country's unification post-independence.

Here's time line of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel :

  • October 31, 1875: Born in Nadiad, Gujarat.
  • 1897: Graduates from the Middle Temple in London with a law degree.
  • 1900: Starts his law practice in Ahmedabad.
  • 1917: Joins the Indian National Congress and begins his political career.
  • 1920: Actively participates in the Non-Cooperation Movement led by Mahatma Gandhi.
  • 1928: Leads the Bardoli Satyagraha, successfully challenging increased land revenue assessments, earning the title "Sardar."
  • 1931: Represents the Congress at the second Round Table Conference in London.
  • 1942: Supports the Quit India Movement, advocating for immediate independence.
  • 1947: India gains independence; Patel becomes the first Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Home Affairs.
  • 1948: Spearheads the integration of over 500 princely states into the Indian Union, emphasizing diplomacy and political negotiation.
  • 1950: Dies in Mumbai.
  • 2018: The Statue of Unity, the world's tallest statue, is inaugurated in his honor in Gujarat.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel is remembered as a key architect of modern India, known for his leadership, vision, and determination in uniting the nation.
